When Do You Need A Structural Engineer?

A practical guide for owners and project teams planning renovations, additions or repairs.

You should usually involve a structural engineer when a project changes the parts of a building that carry loads or keep the structure stable.

Common Triggers

  • Removing or modifying load-bearing walls
  • Adding a story, rear extension or roof bulkhead
  • Changing beams, columns, joists or foundations
  • Repairing cracks, settlement or structural damage
  • Planning work that requires code review or permit documentation

Early engineering review often helps owners avoid redesign, delays and field surprises later in the process.
